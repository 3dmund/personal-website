About Me

Hey, what's up, hello

I'm a sophmore and declared computer science major at UC Berkeley. Computer science is a passion of mine, and I find it amazing what one can do with it. What's even more amazing is technology's growing importance in today's society; my lifelong dream is to make my mark on this technological revolution through my own startup.

I just finished taking my favorite class ever, CS61B, a class on data structures. I loved this class because it introduced me to the actual engineering aspect of computer science. I enjoyed it so much, that ever since, I have been looking for ways to build my own projects outside the classroom. Recently, I have gotten into web design/development, having created a dance website for my sister and this, my personal website. This website has been my favorite project so far because I have learned so much from it and it gives me a medium to showcase both my abilities and me as a person to the world. I've just started picking up app development as well, and I'm looking forward to submitting an app to the app store in the near future.

I believe that everyone has an amazing life story, and I would love to hear about yours. Feel free to message me through the contact form or connect with me through one of the social media icons at the bottom of the page. The down arrow to the right will take you right there!

Split

This is an ios app I am finishing up with two other people using Swift, XCode, and Firebase. The app is purposed to facilitate the purchasing of communal items within a group of people, such as roommates. It includes a shared list where everyone can add and upvote items (like in reddit). The app can also calculate how much each person owes that user, and displays it in the payment feed. It supports Facebook login.
https://github.com/3dmund/Editor

Personal Website

Text Editor

Bear Maps

I developed the backend of a mapping application of Berkeley using Java, Apache Maven, and images downloaded from OpenStreetMap. It can handle features such as moving the screen by dragging, zooming in/out, and double clicking two points to find the shortest path. https://github.com/3dmund/Bear-Maps
